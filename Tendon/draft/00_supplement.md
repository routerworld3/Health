Before the chart: these are general, widely-used supplement dose ranges for a typical adult with a garden-variety tendon ache — not a personal prescription. The safety notes matter as much as the numbers, so don't skip them. And the usual gate: kidney stones, blood thinners, pregnancy, diabetes, kidney disease, or a fresh tear/post-surgery = clear it with a doctor first.

## Safe daily dosage + timing

| Supplement | Safe daily dose | When to take it | Why that timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Collagen peptides** | 15 g (up to ~20 g is fine) | **45 min before** your tendon exercises | Puts building blocks in your blood right as exercise opens the tendon's delivery window |
| **Vitamin C** | 250–500 mg (no need to exceed 500) | Same time as collagen — before exercise | It's the cofactor that assembles collagen; pair them |
| **Omega-3 (EPA+DHA)** | 1–2 g | With any meal that has fat | Fat-soluble; food improves absorption and prevents fishy burps |
| **Magnesium** | 200–400 mg | **Evening / bedtime** | Can aid sleep/muscle relaxation; splitting from other minerals aids absorption |
| **Vitamin D** | 1,000–2,000 IU (only if low — test first) | With a fatty meal | Fat-soluble; better absorbed with food |
| **Zinc** | 8–11 mg (don't exceed ~40 mg) | With food, **not** at the same time as magnesium if possible | Empty-stomach zinc causes nausea; competes with magnesium/iron for uptake |

## A simple daily rhythm

- **Morning/with breakfast:** omega-3, vitamin D (if you take it), zinc
- **~45 min before exercise session:** collagen + vitamin C, then do your loading exercises
- **Evening/bedtime:** magnesium

If you don't exercise on a given day, still take the collagen + vitamin C with any meal — timing is a bonus, not a hard requirement on rest days.

## The safety ceilings worth knowing (plain terms)

- **Vitamin C:** above ~2,000 mg/day risks stomach upset and, if you're prone, kidney stones. You need nowhere near that — 500 is plenty. More does *not* build more collagen.
- **Zinc:** the sneaky one. Chronic high zinc (above ~40 mg/day) depletes your copper and can backfire. Stay at the low end; skip it entirely if your diet has meat/nuts/legumes.
- **Magnesium:** too much just gives you loose stools — uncomfortable, not dangerous. If that happens, lower the dose. (People with kidney disease are the real exception — doctor first.)
- **Vitamin D:** the one you shouldn't guess on. It's fat-soluble and *accumulates*, so megadosing is genuinely risky. This is why "test first" — don't take those 60,000 IU weekly sachets on a hunch.
- **Omega-3:** high doses can thin blood slightly; relevant only if you're on blood thinners or heading into surgery.
- **Collagen:** essentially no toxicity ceiling; worst case is mild fullness. Safe part of the stack.

## The one reminder that matters most

None of these are the treatment — the **progressive loading exercises are**, and these just support them. Give the whole thing 8–12 weeks; tendons are genuinely slow, and no dose makes them fast. If pain is sharp, worsening, or there was a sudden "pop"/injury, that's a stop-and-see-a-doctor signal, not a supplement situation.
