A quick but important flag before the how-to: this is general educational information, not medical advice, and it assumes a garden-variety tendon *ache* (tendinopathy), not a fresh acute tear or post-surgery situation — those need a doctor's specific say-so before you take anything or load the tendon. Also check with a doctor/pharmacist if you're on blood thinners, have kidney stones (vitamin C matters there), or are pregnant. With that said, here's the plain-English version.

## The simple version

**What to take:** Two things that work together — collagen and vitamin C.

| Supplement | How much | The layman reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydrolyzed collagen** (also called collagen peptides) | **15 grams** (about 1–2 scoops of powder, check the label) | The raw building material your tendon uses to repair itself |
| **Vitamin C** | **Somewhere in the 75–500 mg range** — even a small dose does the job | The "glue helper" — your body literally can't assemble collagen without it |

You can buy collagen peptide powder that already has vitamin C mixed in, which saves you buying two things.

## The part that actually matters: TIMING

This is the trick most people miss. **Take it 30–60 minutes BEFORE you do your tendon exercises** (physical therapy, heel drops, whatever your loading program is).

Here's why in plain terms: a tendon has almost no blood flow, so it's hard to deliver repair material to it. But when you *exercise* the tendon, you force blood into the area for a short window. So you want the collagen already floating in your bloodstream *right when* the exercise opens that delivery window. Swallow it → wait ~45 min → do your exercises → the building blocks arrive exactly when the door is open.

So the daily rhythm looks like:

1. **~45 min before your tendon exercises:** mix 15 g collagen + vitamin C in water/juice, drink it.
2. **Do your loading exercises** (this is the actual healing driver — the supplement is just support).
3. That's it for the "timed" dose.

## How long

Give it **8–12 weeks minimum**. Tendons are slow — this is not a days-thing, it's a months-thing. The research protocols ran that long, and so should your expectations.

## The one thing to keep straight

The **exercises are the medicine; the supplement is the helper.** In every study that showed a benefit, people were doing progressive loading exercises AND taking collagen — never the supplement alone sitting on the couch. If you only do one thing, do the exercises. The collagen just makes them work a bit faster.

The good news on safety: across the studies, no side effects were reported for collagen + vitamin C, which is a big part of why it's a reasonable low-risk thing to try — unlike the herbal blend we looked at, which makes big "cures tendons" claims with no human trials behind them.

One honest caveat: even for collagen + vitamin C, the human evidence says "helps speed things up," not "guaranteed fix" — some studies are small. It's a sensible, safe add-on, not a miracle.
